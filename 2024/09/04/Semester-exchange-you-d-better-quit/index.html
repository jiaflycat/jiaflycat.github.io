Semester exchange, you'd better quit!

2024-09-04 · 3 min · 568 words

Reasons You Should Consider Quitting an Exchange Program

TL;DR: Exchange = Long-term Travel

Reason 1: Cost

There are only very few funded exchange programs held by the schools. Typically they are not schools with top reputation and there are very few position. However, tuition-free exchange programs are worth considering. All you need is free time.
Sadly, most top-tier universities like UCB, UPenn only have paid exchange programs. They are really expensive in courses, typically $2250 - $3000 per course. And what you get are merely access to exams and a transcript. The knowledge itself is already open to all the world, as well as programming assignments. So if you would like to attend courses, you can literally save 100% but still learn the same by studying at home, reading materials and finishing homework from the website.

Reason 2: Time

You have to admit that the most priceless treasure in undergraduate is time.Without free time, you couldn’t make any difference. Research, internship, contest… all these proved works need free time.
However, exchange means starting over in a different country. You will waste lot of time on trivial but time-consuming tasks like visa, airplane, rent, cooking, selecting courses, getting rejecting, selecting other courses, studying courses that don’t match your future plan…
These stuff would greatly waste your free time for meaningful work.

Reason 3: Research chances

You may want to find some research intern in labs during the exchange program. However this is nearly impossible. First you need to spend much time on CSRanking or reading their homepages, in order to find some professor that matches. You may read their papers since there’s some new fields. Then you need to write emails. And they may not reply, since you are an exchange student, everyone expect you to leave the country within very short period of time (including you). This is especially true for CS major.
Even if they decided to admit you, they can still withdraw admission anytime.

Reason 4: Potential Risks

Anyway, you are going to live in a brand new environment on your own. You are not familiar with local laws, regulations and procedures, so it’s very likely to make mistakes. Be careful.
Watch out for danger. You are naked in the eyes of evil people in dark. The society is treacherous, you’ll face danger if you don’t have your defenses up.
Make sure you are fully aware of all types of crime: scam, thefts, robbery, carding, fraudulent marketing. Keep an eye on recent alerts from the authority, banks and schools, especially those written in the same language as yours.
Ask yourself before any action like transferring money, going strange places: Have I completely eliminated any possible threats? Have I read through all alerts about scam before sending money? There is no way to get the money back.

You can still consider exchange if…

Your field are less competitive as CS, like EE, ECE, EEE, Maths, Physics. There are much more opportunities in these fields and they lack people during summer. Most importantly, there isn’t much local students as well as visiting student who are apply the same position with you.
Or, you’ve already got in touch with your professor through a course, and you are granted with a research internship. In this case, it is very likely that you will do some meaningful work and get a return offer.
